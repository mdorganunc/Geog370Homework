In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

For each map, I looked up the name or EPSG in the Project Properties/CRS tab. I then applied the new projection. After that, I used the indicatrix plugin to create Tissots Indicatrox circles, which helps visualize the distortion present on each map. I then exported each map as an image and uploaded it to this site. I also was able to export layers and reproject them in my desired CRS. Another option would be to right click the layer, and select the "Set Layer CRS" tab.

WGS84 Projection

In this projection, I am noting quite a bit of distortion around the poles, as indicated by the larger circles on the indicatrix. Shape is preserved, but area is distorted.

Aitoff Projection

The curved shape of this projection helps moderate the distortion around the poles, although some is still present. There is also distortion as you move away from the prime meridian toward the edges of the map. Size here is somewhat more accurate, as seen by the larger size of Africa than Greenland (compared to WSG84)

Now, you should add the following projections on your own:

EPSG: 3857, 53018, 54034, 54027, 102016, and two additional projections that you choose.

Psuedo-Mercator Projection

Large amounts of distortion around the poles (look at the size of Antarctica!), but preserves space and direction.

Sphere Winkel 1 Projection

Curved/Spherical shape of map helps offset distortion. Some size distortion as one moves toward poles/away froom prime meridian, but less size distortion than Mercator.

Equal Area Cylindrical Projection

Noticeable stretching/flattening occuring near poles. Circles occupy more E/W space as you move away from equator. Seemingly Truer representation of size than Mercator.

Equidistant Conic Projection

View has shifted to be from the pole perspective- increasing size/distance distortion as we move away from pole.

Azimuthal Equidistant

View has shifted to be from the pole perspective- increasing size/distance distortion as we move away from pole. This projection even more distorted than previous. Maintains true direction.

Robinson Projection

Distortion around poles, accounts for size better than Mercator. Curved indicatrix graticule grid.

Gall Stereographic Projection

Shape preserved, rectangular grid. LOTS of distortion in size around poles.
